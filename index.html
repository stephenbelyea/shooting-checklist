Shooting Checklist

Skype Calls

Source (that's you):

It's always best to make sure your own setup is tip-top before your scheduled call, so be sure to set some time aside beforehand.

Guest (that's them).

Obviously, you won't have as much control over the guest's shot. Everybody wants to look good on camera, though, so don't be afraid to offer some guidance.

While shooting...

Here's a couple of things to keep in mind while you're talking.

  • Watch your eyeline. If you need to look away, do it with your eyes and keep your head pointed at the camera.
  • Careful with that mouse. Keep the recording screen isolated.
  • Sit up straight. Don't slouch.
  • Speak into the mic, and keep your voice steady. Watch the excited yelling or low mumbling.
  • Slow it down. Take your time and get the words out.
  • Let the guest speak. Your audience likes you, but they're tuning in to hear what the other person has to say.
  • If they're speaking too much or lost the train of thought, step in and help out. No one likes looking foolish on camera.
  • If you flub a line or mumble a question, repeat it. Pause and repeat the whole thing - not just half of the sentence.
  • Same goes if the guest messes up. Let them try it again.
  • If something goes wrong, don't sweat it. Take a second, stop recording if you need to. Get back to it with a clear head.
  • Always leave padding. Give it space after hitting "record" and before hitting "stop".
